A very common question among aviation fans is exactly **Eurofighter Typhoon which country** actually built it. Unlike the F-16 (American) or the Rafale (French), the Typhoon is not the product of a single nation. It is a collaborative European effort.

## The Four Core Nations

The Eurofighter Typhoon was developed to meet the shared air defence needs of four major European powers during the late Cold War period. 
- **United Kingdom:** Represented by BAE Systems. The UK was a primary driver for the aircraft's interceptor and air-superiority capabilities.
- **Germany:** Represented by Airbus (formerly DASA). Germany required an agile fighter to replace their aging fleet.
- **Italy:** Represented by Leonardo (formerly Alenia Aermacchi). 
- **Spain:** Represented by Airbus (formerly CASA). 

## The Eurofighter Consortium

To manage this massive, complex project, the countries formed a joint holding company called Eurofighter Jagdflugzeug GmbH, headquartered in Bavaria, Germany. Production is shared among the partners. Parts built in the UK are shipped to Germany, Italy, and Spain, and vice-versa, with final assembly lines located in all four nations. 

## Why Did France Leave?

Initially, France was part of the early discussions for a unified European fighter. However, disagreements over the aircraft's primary role (France wanted a lighter aircraft capable of carrier operations, while the others wanted a heavy interceptor) led France to leave the programme and develop the Dassault Rafale independently.
