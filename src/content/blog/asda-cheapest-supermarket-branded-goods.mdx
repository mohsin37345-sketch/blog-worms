# Asda Named Cheapest Supermarket for Branded Goods - What the Which? March 2026 Data Shows

**Quick answer:** Which? named Asda the cheapest supermarket for branded groceries for the second month running in its March 2026 analysis. Asda's basket of 241 popular branded items - including brands such as Heinz, Nescafé and Mr Kipling - came to £813.16. That was cheaper than Tesco with Clubcard (£821.39), Sainsbury's with Nectar (£845.69) and Morrisons with a More card (£858.67). This does not mean Asda is always the cheapest supermarket for every basket.

If you have searched for the cheapest supermarket for branded goods, you are probably trying to work out where your regular branded shop costs least. The short answer for March 2026 is Asda - but only for the specific branded-goods basket that Which? tracked. For a smaller mixed basket, Aldi came out cheapest. The full picture depends on what you buy, whether you use loyalty cards, and how you shop.

## Why is Asda being called the cheapest supermarket for branded goods?

Which?, the consumer group, runs monthly supermarket price comparisons across the major UK chains. In its March 2026 analysis, Which? compared 241 popular branded products - the sort of well-known brands most UK shoppers recognise on the shelves.

Asda's basket of those 241 branded items came to £813.16. That made Asda the cheapest for branded groceries for the second consecutive month.

The key finding: Asda's branded prices were available to every shopper without needing a loyalty card or membership. At Tesco and Sainsbury's, non-members paid considerably more.

## What did Which? compare?

Which? tracked a basket of 241 popular branded grocery items across the major supermarkets during March 2026. The basket included well-known brands such as Heinz, Nescafé and Mr Kipling.

The comparison covered Asda, Tesco, Sainsbury's, Morrisons and Waitrose. It included both loyalty-card prices (where relevant) and standard non-member prices.

Aldi and Lidl were **not** included in this branded-goods comparison. The reason is straightforward: they do not stock enough of the same 241 branded products to make a fair comparison. Aldi and Lidl focus more on own-label ranges, so they cannot be measured against the same branded basket.

This is an important distinction. Aldi and Lidl may well be cheaper overall, but they were not part of this specific branded-goods study.

## How did Asda compare with Tesco, Sainsbury's and Morrisons?

Here is what the Which? March 2026 branded-goods comparison found:

The gap between Asda and non-member prices at Tesco and Sainsbury's is striking. A shopper who does not use Clubcard or Nectar could pay well over £100 more for the same branded basket.

## Does this mean Asda is the cheapest supermarket overall?

No. The branded-goods comparison is only one way to measure supermarket prices.

Which? also runs a separate comparison using a smaller basket of 95 grocery items - a mix of branded and own-brand products. In March 2026, that smaller basket produced a very different ranking:

Asda came fourth in this smaller basket - behind Aldi and Lidl. The two discount supermarkets were over £20 cheaper.

So the cheapest supermarket depends entirely on **what** you are buying. Branded goods? Asda came top. A smaller mixed basket? Aldi won.

## What is Asda's cheap brand called?

Asda's budget own-brand range is called **Just Essentials by Asda**. It is Asda's lowest-price tier, covering basic essentials across food, household and personal-care products.

Just Essentials is a separate thing from branded groceries. The Which? comparison that named Asda cheapest was specifically about branded items - products from manufacturers such as Heinz, Nescafé and Mr Kipling. Just Essentials products were not part of that branded-goods basket.

If you are looking for the absolute cheapest products at Asda, the Just Essentials range is where to start. But that is a different question from which supermarket is cheapest for branded goods.

## Branded goods vs own-brand: what is the difference?

**Branded goods** are products made by well-known manufacturers and sold under their own brand names. Think Heinz baked beans, Nescafé coffee, Mr Kipling cakes or Kellogg's cornflakes. These products look the same whichever supermarket you buy them from.

**Own-brand goods** are products made for (or by) the supermarket and sold under the store's own label. Asda has its own-brand range, Tesco has its own, and so on. These are almost always cheaper than the branded equivalent.

**Budget own-brand** ranges sit below the standard own-brand. Asda's is Just Essentials. Tesco has Everyday Value (now part of its standard own-brand tiers). Sainsbury's has its own budget tier too.

Shoppers who switch from branded to own-brand products often save the most. But many people prefer specific branded products for taste, quality or habit - and for those shoppers, the branded-goods comparison is what matters.

## Why do loyalty cards matter?

Three of the major UK supermarkets now offer loyalty-card pricing:

- **Tesco Clubcard** - members see lower prices on selected products throughout the store.
- **Sainsbury's Nectar** - Nectar cardholders get reduced prices on some items.
- **Morrisons More** - a newer scheme offering member-only prices on certain products.

Asda does not operate a traditional loyalty card in the same way. Its prices in the Which? branded-goods comparison were available to everyone.

In the March 2026 branded-goods analysis, Asda beat all three loyalty schemes. But the gap was smallest with Tesco Clubcard (about 1%). Without a loyalty card, Tesco and Sainsbury's shoppers paid substantially more - up to 15% more than Asda for the same basket.

Loyalty cards are free to sign up for, so most regular shoppers at those stores will use them. But some people prefer not to share their shopping data. If you shop at Tesco or Sainsbury's without a loyalty card, you may be paying more than you realise.

## Is Asda a cheap store?

Asda is generally one of the more competitive big supermarkets in the UK. In Which?'s branded-goods comparisons, Asda has come out cheapest for two consecutive months. In the smaller-basket comparison, Asda typically finishes behind Aldi and Lidl but ahead of the other big chains.

Asda is not a discounter like Aldi or Lidl. It is a full-range supermarket with branded goods, own-brand products, fresh counters, clothing and more. For shoppers who want to buy branded products from a full-range store, Asda has been the cheapest option in recent months according to Which?.

Whether Asda is "cheap" for you depends on your basket.

## Key background

UK supermarket price comparisons are published regularly by Which? and other consumer groups. They give shoppers a snapshot of how prices compare at a particular point in time.

Different types of comparison produce different winners. A branded-goods basket measures the cost of well-known brands across supermarkets. A smaller mixed basket includes both branded and own-brand items. Each tells a different story.

Loyalty-card pricing has changed how comparisons work. Tesco, Sainsbury's and Morrisons now offer member-only prices on many items. A shopper with Clubcard sees different shelf prices than a non-member. That means a single supermarket can appear in two positions in the same comparison - one price for members, another for everyone else.

Discount supermarkets such as Aldi and Lidl do not carry the full range of branded products. They compete mainly on own-label goods and a limited branded selection. That makes them hard to compare directly on a 241-item branded basket - but they often rank first or second for smaller, mixed baskets.

Prices change month to month. Supermarkets adjust pricing in response to wholesale costs, competitor moves, seasonal demand and promotional cycles. A supermarket that is cheapest in March may not hold that position in April.
