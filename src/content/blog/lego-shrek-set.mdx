## What is the LEGO Shrek set

This is the key detail to understand. This is not just a rumor or a fan concept making the rounds online. This is the first ever official LEGO Shrek release produced in direct collaboration with Universal Products and Experiences. To properly celebrate the 25th anniversary of the franchise, the LEGO Group has unveiled two entirely separate models.

The primary attraction is a substantial, highly detailed display model designed specifically for older fans and seasoned builders. Accompanying this flagship release is a more accessible, character focused set belonging to the popular BrickHeadz line. Together, they represent a long awaited expansion into the DreamWorks Animation catalog.

## LEGO Shrek release date and preorder details

Here is what fans need to know regarding availability. Following the massive global reveal on 23 April 2026, the company confirmed that the official LEGO Shrek release date is firmly locked for 1 June 2026. This summer launch window positions the sets perfectly for the mid year building season.

If you are eager to secure your copy, the good news is that the LEGO Shrek preorder is live right now. ## LEGO Shrek price, piece count, and age rating

Understanding the scale of these sets helps explain their positioning in the market. The main display model, carrying the product number 72423, is a robust build containing 1,403 pieces. It carries an 18+ age rating, clearly signaling its status as a complex display piece rather than a traditional play toy. Its official price is set at £109.99 in the UK, $129.99 in the US, and €119.99 across Europe.

The secondary set, the LEGO Shrek BrickHeadz bearing product number 40923, offers a much smaller commitment. It contains 259 pieces and carries a 10+ age rating, making it suitable for younger builders and casual fans. This set is priced affordably at £19.99 in the UK, $24.99 in the US, and €24.99 in Europe.

## What characters are included in the LEGO Shrek sets

This is where it gets interesting for character enthusiasts. The character selection has been cleverly divided between the two releases. The larger, premium display set features the iconic trio of Shrek, Donkey, and Puss in Boots. These figures are designed to interact perfectly with the detailed environment provided in the box.

Meanwhile, the smaller BrickHeadz set takes a slightly different approach. While it also features Shrek and Donkey in the chunky, stylized BrickHeadz format, it swaps out Puss in Boots for fan favorite Gingy. This smart character distribution encourages dedicated fans to pick up both sets if they want to complete their core character collection.

## LEGO Shrek 72423 explained

What makes this set special is the incredible attention to environmental detail. Officially titled LEGO Shrek: Shrek, Donkey and Puss in Boots, this 1,403 piece model is a love letter to the original film. It is designed primarily as a posable display set, allowing owners to recreate iconic moments perfectly on their shelves.

The build focuses heavily on capturing the muddy, vibrant essence of the swamp. Builders will construct a detailed swamp base complete with the legendary Beware Ogre sign. The designers have packed the environment with brilliant Easter eggs, including a strategically placed onion, bright sunflowers, and even a blue flower with red thorns. It is exactly the kind of deep cut referencing that adult collectors deeply appreciate.

## LEGO Shrek 40923 BrickHeadz explained

For those who want a smaller footprint or a more budget friendly option, the BrickHeadz offering is excellent. This 259 piece set delivers Shrek, Donkey, and Gingy in the unmistakable blocky proportions that define the BrickHeadz aesthetic. It is a fantastic, quick build that looks perfect sitting on an office desk or nestled on a small bookshelf.

The inclusion of Gingy is a massive selling point here, offering a charming recreation of the beloved gingerbread man that balances out the larger green presence of the titular ogre. It is an ideal gift for a casual fan who might find a massive 1,403 piece display set slightly overwhelming.
