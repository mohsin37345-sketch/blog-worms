## Who Is John Ternus?

John Ternus is one of Apple's most senior and influential executives. Before being named CEO, he served as **Senior Vice President of Hardware Engineering** — a role that placed him at the very heart of Apple's product development machine.

Born and raised in the United States, Ternus joined Apple in the early 2000s and steadily climbed through the ranks of the company's hardware division. His engineering background and deep understanding of Apple's product ecosystem made him a natural choice for the top job.

### Career Timeline at Apple

| Year | Role |
|------|------|
| Early 2000s | Joined Apple as hardware engineer |
| 2013 | Vice President of Hardware Engineering |
| 2021 | Senior Vice President of Hardware Engineering |
| 2026 | Named CEO of Apple |

Unlike some tech CEOs who come from finance or marketing backgrounds, Ternus is a **product person through and through**. This is significant because it signals Apple's continued commitment to hardware innovation as the cornerstone of its business strategy.

## What Has John Ternus Built at Apple?

Ternus's fingerprints are on virtually every major Apple product released in the last two decades. Here's a look at the key products he's helped shape:

### iPhone Revolution

As part of the hardware engineering leadership, Ternus played a crucial role in the evolution of the iPhone from a single product to a family of devices. His team was responsible for the engineering challenges behind features like:

- **Face ID** technology and the TrueDepth camera system
- The transition to **in-house Apple Silicon** in iPhones
- Advanced camera systems including ProRAW and Cinematic mode
- **Ceramic Shield** display technology
- The innovative **Dynamic Island** interface

### Mac and Apple Silicon

Perhaps Ternus's most visible public moment came during the Apple Silicon transition. He was the executive who presented the **M1, M2, and M3 chip families** and their integration into Mac hardware at Apple keynotes. This transition from Intel processors to Apple's own chips was one of the most significant engineering achievements in modern computing history.

### Apple Vision Pro

Ternus was instrumental in bringing the **Apple Vision Pro** mixed reality headset to market — a product that represents Apple's biggest new product category in years. The complexity of engineering a spatial computing device with hundreds of sensors and displays made this one of Apple's most ambitious projects ever.

## Tim Cook's Legacy at Apple

To understand the significance of this transition, it's worth reflecting on what Tim Cook achieved during his tenure as CEO:

Tim Cook took over from Steve Jobs in **August 2011** and transformed Apple into the world's most valuable company. Under his leadership:

- Apple's **market capitalisation** grew from approximately $350 billion to over **$3 trillion**
- Revenue more than doubled, with services becoming a major revenue stream
- The company launched **Apple Watch**, **AirPods**, **HomePod**, and **Apple Vision Pro**
- Apple made significant commitments to **privacy**, **sustainability**, and **accessibility**
- The supply chain became one of the most efficient operations in global business

Cook's decision to step down appears to be part of a carefully planned succession strategy, with industry insiders suggesting he will remain on Apple's board of directors in an advisory capacity.
