## What Is Happening with Morrisons Store Closures

Morrisons is closing 17 of its smaller convenience stores, which traded under the Morrisons Daily brand. These are not full size supermarkets. They are the compact neighbourhood shops that Morrisons launched to compete with the likes of Tesco Express and Sainsbury's Local.

The company confirmed the closures after a review of store performance across its convenience estate. Some of these stores were not generating enough footfall or revenue to justify keeping them open, particularly given the rising costs of rent, staffing, and supply chain logistics.

This follows an earlier round of closures. Morrisons chose to close additional stores after the previous closure of 16 in April. The current wave represents the final batch in what appears to be a broader pullback from the convenience sector.

It is worth noting that these Morrisons latest closures affect only the smaller format stores. The main Morrisons supermarkets are not part of this closure programme.

## Why Is Morrisons Closing Stores

The reasons behind the Morrisons restructuring are not unusual in the current retail landscape. Several factors have contributed to this decision.

**Store performance:** Some Morrisons Daily locations simply were not attracting enough customers. The convenience store market in the UK is extremely competitive, with established players like Co-op, Tesco Express, and Sainsbury's Local already holding strong positions.

**Rising costs:** Rent, energy bills, wages, and supply chain costs have all increased significantly. For smaller stores with lower turnover, these costs can quickly make a location unprofitable.

**Strategic refocus:** Under its current ownership, Morrisons appears to be focusing its investment on its core supermarket estate rather than spreading resources across a large number of smaller convenience outlets.

**Debt management:** Since the 2021 leveraged buyout by CD&R, Morrisons has been carrying substantial debt. Closing underperforming stores is one way to reduce ongoing losses and redirect capital more efficiently.

There have also been wider reports about internal changes at Morrisons, including stories about management restructuring. Searches for topics like "Morrisons store manager sacked" reflect the broader anxiety among staff about job security during this period of change. Morrisons has said it will try to find alternative roles for affected employees, but some Morrisons jobs at risk are inevitable when stores close permanently.

## Is Morrisons Supermarket in Financial Trouble

This is a question that requires a careful and honest answer. Morrisons is facing financial pressures, but it is not on the verge of collapse.

The takeover by Clayton, Dubilier and Rice in 2021 was a leveraged buyout, meaning a significant portion of the purchase price was funded by debt. That debt sits on the company's balance sheet and must be serviced through regular interest payments. When combined with thinning profit margins and a tough consumer spending environment, this creates genuine financial strain.

However, Morrisons remains one of the UK's largest supermarket chains with hundreds of stores, thousands of employees, and a well known brand. The Morrisons supermarket closures UK that have been announced are limited to the convenience format. The company continues to trade and invest in its larger stores.

It would be inaccurate to say Morrisons is in financial trouble in the sense of being about to go under. It would also be misleading to say everything is fine. The reality sits somewhere in between. The company is managing a heavy debt load while trying to remain competitive, and the convenience store closures are part of that adjustment.

## Is Morrisons Owned by Tesco

No. This is a common misconception that comes up frequently in search results, but it is completely incorrect.

Morrisons is not owned by Tesco. They are entirely separate companies. Morrisons was founded in 1899 in Bradford and grew into one of the UK's "Big Four" supermarkets alongside Tesco, Sainsbury's, and Asda.

In October 2021, Morrisons was acquired by Clayton, Dubilier and Rice, commonly known as CD&R. This is an American private equity firm. The deal was worth around £7 billion and took Morrisons off the stock market, making it a privately held company.

Tesco is the UK's largest supermarket chain and operates independently. The confusion may arise because both brands are so well known, or because of past speculation about potential mergers in the grocery sector. But to be clear: Morrisons and Tesco have no ownership connection whatsoever.
