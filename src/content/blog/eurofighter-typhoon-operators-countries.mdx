While the aircraft was built by four European nations, several other air forces around the world have purchased it. If you are wondering about the **countries that use the Eurofighter Typhoon**, here is the current verified list of operators.

## The Core European Operators

The original four partner nations formed the backbone of the production run and remain the largest operators:
1. **United Kingdom (Royal Air Force):** The UK uses the Typhoon extensively for Quick Reaction Alert (QRA), air policing, and expeditionary strike missions. They operate heavily upgraded Tranche 2 and 3 variants.
2. **Germany (Luftwaffe):** A major user currently investing in the Tranche 4 "Quadriga" program to replace older Tranche 1 jets with brand new, AESA-radar equipped models.
3. **Italy (Aeronautica Militare):** Italy uses the Typhoon as its primary air defence fighter, operating alongside their F-35 fleet.
4. **Spain (Ejército del Aire):** Spain recently ordered additional new Typhoons under the Halcón program to modernize its fleet.

## Middle Eastern Export Operators

The Eurofighter Typhoon has found massive success in the Middle East:
5. **Saudi Arabia (Royal Saudi Air Force):** The largest export customer, operating over 70 Typhoons with a potential interest in further acquisitions.
6. **Oman (Royal Air Force of Oman):** Operates a small but highly capable fleet of Tranche 3 aircraft.
7. **Kuwait (Kuwait Air Force):** Kuwait received some of the most advanced Typhoons ever built, featuring the very first operational Captor-E AESA radars.
8. **Qatar (Qatar Emiri Air Force):** Qatar recently introduced the Typhoon into service to operate alongside their F-15s and Rafales.

## Other European Operators

9. **Austria (Austrian Air Force):** Austria operates a small fleet of Tranche 1 aircraft solely for airspace policing.
