# How Many Weeks, Terms and Days Are in a School Year in the UK?

Most UK schools have 3 main terms throughout the year. Many schools split these into 6 half-terms by adding a break in the middle of each. In England, pupils usually have 190 school days, which is equal to 38 school weeks. However, the school year is often described as 39 weeks because teacher training days are included. Keep in mind that exact dates and holidays vary by country, local council, and individual school.

Planning childcare and booking family holidays can feel like solving a complicated puzzle. Parents constantly search for clear answers on exactly how long the school year actually is, especially when terms like "190 days," "38 weeks," and "39 weeks" are thrown around by councils and headteachers. Knowing how the academic calendar is built will help you understand when you need cover and when you can take a break. This guide breaks down the simple maths behind the UK school year and explains how dates differ depending on where you live.

## How many weeks are in a school year in the UK?

In England, pupils usually attend school for 190 days, which equals 38 five-day school weeks. However, local councils often describe the school year as 195 days or 39 weeks. This happens because teachers are required to work an extra 5 days for training or INSET (In-Service Training) days. The exact wording and lengths can differ depending on the nation and the specific type of school your child attends.

## How many terms are in a school year?

Most UK schools use 3 main terms. These are the autumn term, the spring term, and the summer term. Because each of these main terms is usually split in the middle by a week-long half-term break, the school year practically functions as 6 shorter blocks of learning.

## How many days are in a school year?

There is a 190 pupil-day pattern across England, Wales, and Scotland for maintained schools. This means children are sitting at their desks for 190 days out of the year. Northern Ireland uses different official wording, stating that schools must open for 200 days, though specific closures and training days mean the pupil experience can vary. Remember that teacher training days mean that teachers work more days than the pupils attend.

## How many weeks are in each school term?

The length of each term varies depending on where holidays land. Here is a typical breakdown for England and Wales:
- **Autumn term:** Around 13 to 14 weeks long, usually split evenly by the October half-term break.
- **Spring term:** Around 11 to 12 weeks long, usually split by the February half-term break.
- **Summer term:** Around 12 to 13 weeks long, usually split by the May half-term break.

The dates of the Easter bank holidays move every year, which regularly changes exactly how many weeks fall into the spring and summer terms.

## How does the UK school year work?

The school year starts around early September in England and Wales. Scotland often starts earlier, usually in mid-August. Northern Ireland usually begins in early September and finishes around late June. The standard calendar rolls through the autumn, spring, and summer terms, separated by half-term breaks in October, February, and May. The longer breaks happen at Christmas, Easter, and during the summer.

## How many weeks are school holidays in the UK?

If pupils attend school for 38 weeks in a 52-week year, that leaves roughly 14 weeks outside of pupil attendance. However, the practical reality for parents is usually around 13 weeks of school holidays, with the remaining time made up of INSET days and weekends.

A typical holiday pattern in England and Wales looks like this:
- **Summer holiday:** Around 6 weeks.
- **Christmas holiday:** Around 2 weeks.
- **Easter holiday:** Around 2 weeks.
- **Autumn half-term:** Around 1 week.
- **February half-term:** Around 1 week.
- **May half-term:** Around 1 week.

Exact holiday dates always vary by local council and individual school.

## What are the UK school holidays for 2026?

There is no single, UK-wide school holiday calendar for 2026. Every local authority sets its own term dates. To find the exact dates for 2026, you must check your local council's website or look directly at your child's school calendar. Dates regularly vary for academies, independent schools, voluntary aided schools, and across Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland.

## Primary school vs secondary school: are the days different?

In many areas, primary and secondary schools follow the exact same term dates set by the local council. The number of required pupil days does not change simply because a school is a primary or a secondary. However, individual schools can choose to place their INSET days on different dates, or an academy might set a slightly different calendar. Always check your specific school calendar rather than assuming it matches a sibling's school.

## Why do some sources say 38 weeks and others say 39 weeks?

You will often see both numbers because they count different things. If you take the 190 days that pupils are in class and divide them by 5 days a week, you get 38 weeks. If you take the 195 days that teachers are paid to work and divide by 5, you get 39 weeks. The difference is the 5 teacher training or INSET days when the school is closed to pupils but open for staff.

## Key background

Understanding the school year helps you manage childcare and family time. A "term" is a major block of learning, while a "half-term" is a shorter block created by a one-week holiday. "INSET days" are when teachers are working but children stay home. While local authorities publish master calendars, academies and independent schools have the freedom to set their own dates, which is why your neighbour's child might have a different day off than yours.

## FAQs about the UK school year

### Are there 3 or 6 terms in a UK school year?
There are usually 3 main terms, but because they are split by half-term breaks, the school calendar practically operates in 6 shorter blocks.

### Is a school year 38 or 39 weeks?
It is 38 weeks of actual attendance for pupils, but 39 weeks for teachers when you include their required training days.

### Do academies follow council term dates?
Many academies choose to follow their local council calendar, but they have the legal freedom to set their own dates, so parents must check with the school directly.

### Do private schools have the same term dates?
Independent schools often set their own unique term dates, and their holidays are usually much longer than those of state-funded schools.

### Why do school holidays change each year?
Holidays shift because the dates of Easter move, bank holidays change, and councils and schools make different decisions about when to schedule INSET days.

### Can I take my child on holiday during term time?
You must check the official school attendance rules and ask the headteacher for permission. If you take an unauthorised holiday, you can be fined.

### Do Scotland school holidays match England?
No, Scotland often starts the school year in August and finishes in June, meaning their summer holiday dates differ significantly from England and Wales.
